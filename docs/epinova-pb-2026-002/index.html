Governing Structural Centrality

Greenland as an AI-Strategic Node under the AI-SNI Framework

  • Wu, Shaoyuan
    Global AI Governance and Policy Research Center, EPINOVA LLC
    https://orcid.org/0009-0008-0660-8232

Description

This policy brief applies the AI-Strategic Node Index (AI-SNI v0.1) to Greenland as an AI-strategic node. It evaluates Greenland’s structural exposure across five AI-SNI dimensions, reports an AI-SNI composite result of 0.52 in the Tier 3 Relevant Exposure Band, identifies Infrastructure–Governance Asymmetry as the binding constraint, and argues that Greenland’s policy challenge is governance stabilization rather than capacity expansion.

Abstract

Greenland has already become a structurally significant node within multiple AI-mediated systems, including strategic early-warning, Arctic surveillance, climate modelling, and emerging resource–data–energy coupling. Application of the AI-Strategic Node Index (AI-SNI) yields a composite score of 0.52 (Tier 3), indicating relevant structural exposure rather than elevated risk or strategic superiority. The diagnostic profile shows high functional centrality in sensing and decision-loop timing, moderate global modelling leverage, latent future optionality, and a critical governance deficit in infrastructure control, auditability, and escalation authority. The brief argues that the primary challenge for structurally central AI nodes is not how to increase strategic importance, but how to govern an importance that already exists. Without targeted governance stabilization, structural centrality may translate into systemic fragility.
