Energy Endurance Under Systemic Shock: Divergent Survival Pathways in East Asia During the U.S.–Israel–Iran Conflict

  • Wu, Shaoyuan
    Global AI Governance and Policy Research Center, EPINOVA LLC
    https://orcid.org/0009-0008-0660-8232

Description

This policy brief examines energy endurance under systemic shock and compares divergent survival pathways in East Asia during the U.S.–Israel–Iran conflict. It focuses on the structural exposure, energy import dependence, reserve-release behavior, and resilience differences among Japan, South Korea, and Taiwan under conditions of geopolitical disruption and regional energy pressure.

Abstract

This policy brief examines how systemic shock in the U.S.–Israel–Iran conflict affects energy endurance in East Asia. Using Japan, South Korea, and Taiwan as comparative cases, the brief analyzes oil and natural gas dependence, reserve-release behavior, supply vulnerability, and differentiated resilience pathways. It argues that energy endurance is not determined solely by import volume, but by the interaction among strategic reserves, diversification capacity, institutional response speed, and exposure to systemic maritime and geopolitical risk.
